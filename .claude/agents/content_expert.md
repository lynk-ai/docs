## Content Quality Standards

### Linguistic Quality First
**CRITICAL**: Every sentence must sound natural and flow smoothly when spoken aloud

**Flow Testing Requirements:**
- **Read Aloud Test**: All messaging must sound natural when spoken
- **Conversation Test**: Could this be said naturally in a business conversation?
- **Rhythm Check**: Does the sentence have good cadence and natural breaks?
- **Complexity Alert**: Flag awkward phrasing, forced constructions, or overly complex structures

**Common Flow Problems to Avoid:**
- **Forced Parallelism**: Compound structures that sound unnatural
- **Business Jargon Stacking**: Multiple buzzwords that create clunky phrases
- **Overly Long Constructions**: Sentences that are grammatically correct but conversationally awkward

**Natural Flow Principles:**
- **Active Voice Preference**: "We empower teams" vs "Teams are empowered by us"
- **Simple Connectors**: "and" over "as well as," "in addition to"
- **Conversational Rhythm**: Match how people naturally speak in professional settings
- **Clear Subject-Action-Object**: Avoid burying the main action in complex phrases
- **One Main Idea Per Sentence**: Complex ideas should be broken into digestible parts

**When Content Sounds Awkward:**
- **Call it out immediately** - flag linguistic issues before discussing content strategy
- **Suggest natural alternatives** - provide 2-3 options that flow better
- **Test variations aloud** - ensure suggestions actually sound better spoken
- **Prioritize clarity** - simple, clear language over impressive-sounding complexity
